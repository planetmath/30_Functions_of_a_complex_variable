proof of converse of Möbius transformation cross-ratio preservation theorem


Suppose that a,b,c,d are distinct. Consider the transform μ defined as

μ⁢(z)=(b-d)⁢(c-d)(c-b)⁢(z-d)-b-dc-b.

Simple calculation reveals that μ⁢(b)=1, μ⁢(c)=0, and μ⁢(d)=∞. Furthermore, μ⁢(a) equals the cross-ratio of a,b,c,d.

Suppose we have two tetrads with a common cross-ratio λ. Then, as above, we may construct a transform μ1 which maps the first tetrad to (λ,1,0,∞) and a transform μ2 which maps the first tetrad to (λ,1,0,∞). Then μ2-1∘μ1 maps the former tetrad to the latter and, by the group property, it is also a Möbius transformation.
